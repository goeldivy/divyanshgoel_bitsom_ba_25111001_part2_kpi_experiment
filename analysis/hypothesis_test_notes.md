# Hypothesis Test Notes

## Business Objective

Determine whether the new onboarding experience (Treatment group) significantly improves the paid conversion rate compared to the existing onboarding experience (Control group).

## Metric Tested

Primary Metric: Paid Conversion Rate

## Null Hypothesis (H0)

There is no significant difference in the paid conversion rate between the Control group and the Treatment group.

## Alternative Hypothesis (H1)

The Treatment group has a significantly higher paid conversion rate than the Control group.

## Test Type

One-tailed hypothesis test.

## Significance Level

α = 0.05

## Decision Rule

If the p-value is less than 0.05, reject the null hypothesis and conclude that the Treatment group performs significantly better.

If the p-value is greater than or equal to 0.05, fail to reject the null hypothesis.

## Business Interpretation

The hypothesis test helps determine whether the observed improvement in paid conversion is statistically meaningful and whether the new onboarding experience should be recommended for deployment.

## Assumptions

* Users were randomly assigned to Control and Treatment groups.
* Observations are independent.
* Sample sizes are sufficient for comparison.
* The experiment was conducted under similar business conditions.
